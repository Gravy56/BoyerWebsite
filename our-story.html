The Boyer Timeline

Nine Decades of Craft

1936

Brothers William & Emil Boyer Found the Company

In Altoona, Pennsylvania, brothers William and Emil Boyer start Boyer Candy Company with a dream of making the finest candy in America. The company begins small, but the ambition is anything but modest.

1936

The Mallo Cup Is Born

Boyer invents the Mallo Cup — a cup of rich milk chocolate filled with a fluffy marshmallow cream center. It immediately becomes the company's flagship product, and remains so nearly 90 years later. Inside each package: a collectible Play Money card.

1983

Boyer Acquires the Clark Bar

Boyer takes ownership of the Clark Bar — a legendary American candy bar first created in Pittsburgh in 1917. Known for its crispy peanut butter and toffee interior, the Clark Bar had been a national staple for decades. Boyer commits to preserving the original recipe.

Today

Still in Altoona. Still the Same.

Every Boyer product is still made in the same Altoona, Pennsylvania facility. The recipes haven't changed. The commitment to quality hasn't changed. And the Play Money cards are still tucked inside every package — waiting to be collected and redeemed.

Altoona, Pennsylvania — Our Home Since 1936
Altoona, Pennsylvania

Rooted in
One Place

Altoona has been the home of Boyer Candy since day one. In a country where manufacturing has moved overseas and supply chains have become unrecognizable, Boyer remains a point of local pride — a factory that employs Altoona residents and ships American-made candy to every corner of the country.

When you buy a Mallo Cup, you're buying something made by real people, in a real American town, using a real recipe that's nearly a century old. That's not nothing. That's everything.
